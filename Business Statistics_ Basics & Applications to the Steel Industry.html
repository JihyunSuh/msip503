Variables and Measurement

Variable: a characteristic that can vary in value among subjects in a sample or population

  • Income last year : $ XXXXXX
  • Number of siblings : 0, 1, 2, 3...
  • Employed or not: YES or NO
  • Gender: Female or Male

Variables have different measurement scales

5 / 53

Variables and Measurement

Measurement scale

  • Quantitative:measurement scale has numerical values that represent different magnitudes of the variable
    • Example: annual income,number of siblings,age, # of years of education completed
  • Qualitative: when measurement scale is a set of categories
    • Example:marital status (single, married ...), nationality, religious affiliation
    • Distinct categories differ in quality not in numerical magnitude (qualitative)

7 / 53

Ratio scale

  • An absolute 'zero' point
    • You can measure the absolute amount of variable
    • Allows to calculate 'ratio'
    • Example: 10 liter of water in a jar has twice as much water as a jar of 5 liter

8 / 53

Interval scale

  • Ordered category with equal intervals
    • Separate adjacent scores and arbitrary zero
    • Example:temperature, IQ score
    • 80 is 40 higher than 40 but not twice as hot as 40

9 / 53

Ordinal scale

  • Consists of a set of categories that are organized in an ordered sequence
  • You can think of ordinal scale as a rank order
    • Example:
    • 1st, 2nd, 3rd...(rank)
    • small, medium, large
    • Bronze, silver, gold medal
    • Never, less than once a month, 1 - 3 times a month, every week, more than once a week

10 / 53

Nominal scale (qualitative)

  • Consists of a set of categories that have different names
  • Do no make any quantitative distinctions between observations
    • Example: Your primary mode of transportation to work
      • Automobile, Bus, Subway, Bicycle, Walking
  • No category is greater than or smaller than any other category

Nominal data: Pie Chart

Advantages of Pie Charts

  • Easy to understand the proportion for each component
  • Allows us to make rough comparisons among different components

Disadvantages of Pie Charts

  • Less useful with a large number of categories or the difference among the components is very small
  • Sometimes, it is misleading the actual differences between the components

Histogram

Advantages of Histogram

  • One of the most widely used format of the plot
  • A single glance at a histogram gives us some idea about the shape and spread of the data
  • Can represent a large number of bars representing different class intervals

29 / 53

Histogram

Disadvantages of Histogram

  • A histogram cannot be used to represent discrete frequency distributions
  • Histograms cannot be used to compare two different data sets
  • While the mode can be calculated using a histogram, the mean and the median cannot be calculated using a histogram

Time Series Data: Line Chart

Advantages of Line Charts

  • Easy to see both short-term and long-term changes
  • Simpler to construct and read than bar graphs or histograms

Disadvantages of Line Charts

  • Does not reveal much about the skewness or kurtosis of the data
  • Can be used to manipulate or deceive the general public (y axis range)

Relationship between two values: Scatter

Advantages of Line Charts

  • are very easy to plot and interpret
  • Outliers are isolated and do not affect the results
  • Shows how strongly two variables are related & direction of the relationship

Disadvantages of Line Charts

  • Only depicts the direction but not the degree of correlation
  • If there are too many points to be plotted then the scatter diagram becomes too cluttered and unreadable
  • Does not show the relationship for more than two variables

Presenting data: Recap

  • What is a good graph?
  1. It presents large data sets concisely and coherently
  2. The viewer can easily understand the message
  3. It should be able to provide relationship between multiple variables
  4. The form of the graph should help viewers can understand the substance of the data
  5. No deception
